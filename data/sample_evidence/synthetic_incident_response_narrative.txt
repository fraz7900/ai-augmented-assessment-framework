Northwind Grid Utility operates a tiered incident response program spanning both information technology and operational technology environments. The Security Operations Center provides continuous monitoring of network telemetry, endpoint detection signals, and OT network sensors deployed at substations and generation facilities. Alerts are triaged within fifteen minutes during business hours and within one hour outside business hours, consistent with the organization's internal service level commitments.

When an event is escalated to an incident, an incident commander is assigned from a standing rotation of trained personnel across security, IT operations, and OT engineering. The incident commander coordinates containment actions, communicates status to affected business units, and determines whether external notification obligations apply, including notification to the relevant NERC regional entity when an incident meets the reportability criteria defined in the organization's regulatory obligations matrix.

Containment procedures differ meaningfully between IT and OT environments. In IT environments, standard containment includes network isolation of affected hosts, credential rotation for compromised accounts, and forensic imaging prior to remediation. In OT environments, containment decisions must additionally weigh operational safety and reliability impact; a control system component is not taken offline without engineering sign-off confirming that doing so will not create an unsafe operating condition or an unplanned reliability event. This IT/OT distinction is treated as a first-class design constraint throughout the incident response program, not an afterthought layered onto an IT-oriented playbook.

Post-incident, a root cause analysis is conducted within ten business days of incident closure. Findings feed into a corrective action tracker reviewed monthly by the Cybersecurity Program Management function, which is also responsible for maintaining alignment between incident response outcomes and the organization's broader cybersecurity capability maturity objectives.

Tabletop exercises simulating both IT-focused and OT-focused incident scenarios are conducted at least twice annually, with participation from engineering, operations, legal, and executive leadership. Exercise findings are documented and tracked to closure using the same corrective action process applied to real incidents, so that simulated and real findings receive equivalent follow-through rather than being treated as a lower-priority category of work.

Information sharing with sector partners, including participation in relevant information sharing and analysis organizations, is coordinated by the threat intelligence function and is treated as a standing input to the risk management program rather than an ad hoc activity performed only after a major incident.
